Concept

Autism is characterized by impairments in communication, behavior and social interaction. Currently, there is no known cure for autism. However, as quoted by the National Autism Association, “with early intervention and treatment, the diverse symptoms related to autism can be greatly improved and in some cases completely overcome.“

As part of our course work at Cornell University, our team of 5 students took on the grand challenge of addressing issues faced by individuals with autism, in particular for children in their early years of development.

I contributed to the design of Autino, a responsive ecosystem that creates adaptive games, collects data, gives actionable feedback and provides critical sensory intervention for autistic children. I created a prototype for reporting data that would enable caregivers, parents and clinicians to plan and execute effective and evidence-based interventions.

Process

  • Research Existing Solutions

    Researched existing solutions under three major categories – Smartwear, Applications, Wearables.

  • Identify Shortcomings

    - Lack of evidence-based interventions
    - Challenges in data collection
    - Lack of continuous interventions
    - Lack of unobtrusive sensory interventions

  • Analyze Problem Space

    Processed information gathered from relevant research, academic papers and commercial products.

  • Designed and Brainstormed Interventions

    Gathered information for three main stakeholders- caregivers, parents and clinicians, on the type of assessment (Response to name, Vocabulary, Visual performance, Imitation, Reception & Expression), measure of response to each of these assessments and the level of response detail appreciated by each stakeholder.
